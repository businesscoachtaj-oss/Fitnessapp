Scientific Foundation

Why This Works

📈
Progressive Overload via Volume Periodisation
Priority muscles start at Minimum Effective Volume (MEV) in Week 1 and climb toward Maximum Adaptive Volume (MAV) by Week 6. Volume is then pushed to Maximum Recoverable Volume (MRV) in Week 7 before a structured deload. This wave-loading prevents accommodation and forces consistent adaptation.
🎯
Frequency Matching Muscle Recovery Rate
Priority muscles are trained 2–3× per week — the frequency window supported by Schoenfeld et al. for maximising protein synthesis. Traps and side delts recover in 24–36 hours (small muscles, high fatigue tolerance), so they're hit more often. Glutes and lower back are given 48h minimum between sessions.
💪
RIR-Based Intensity Progression
All working sets are prescribed in Reps in Reserve (RIR) rather than fixed percentages. Research by Zourdos et al. shows RIR-based autoregulation produces superior hypertrophy because it accounts for daily fluctuations in performance. RIR progresses from RIR 3–4 (Phase 1) down to RIR 0–1 (Phase 4).
🔁
Deload Is Not Optional
Week 8 is a structured deload — not a rest week. Volume drops to ~50%, load stays the same. This allows connective tissue recovery, replenishes muscle glycogen, and supercompensation peaks in the days immediately post-deload — timing your next program start perfectly. Skipping it forfeits 10–20% of your adaptation gains.
Priority Muscle Analysis

5 Targets. One Plan.

01
Traps
3× / Week
Upper and mid traps. Trained with shrugs, face pulls, rack pulls and upright rows. High frequency tolerated due to fast recovery. Direct trap work added to both pull days and one push day.
W1–2: 12 sets W5–6: 18 sets W7: 20 sets
02
Side Delts
3× / Week
The side (lateral) head of the deltoid. Lateral raises, cable variations and upright rows. Side delts require high rep ranges (15–25) and very high frequency to grow — they're notoriously stubborn and need consistent tension stimulus.
W1–2: 12 sets W5–6: 18 sets W7: 21 sets
03
Glutes
2× / Week
Glute max and glute med. Hip thrusts, RDLs, split squats and abduction work. Lower frequency than upper body muscles due to systemic fatigue cost. Two dedicated glute sessions per week with 48h minimum between them.
W1–2: 10 sets W5–6: 16 sets W7: 18 sets
04
Lower Back
2× / Week
Spinal erectors and multifidus. Trained through hyperextensions, good mornings and Romanian deadlifts. Slow-twitch dominant — responds best to moderate-high reps with time under tension. Conservative frequency due to CNS load.
W1–2: 8 sets W5–6: 14 sets W7: 16 sets
05
Abs & Core
4× / Week
Rectus abdominis, obliques and TVA. High frequency is ideal — abs recover rapidly. Mix of weighted flexion (cable crunch, decline crunch), anti-rotation (Pallof press) and anti-extension (ab wheel) to develop all three functional roles.
W1–2: 12 sets W5–6: 20 sets W7: 22 sets

Periodisation

4 Phases, 8 Weeks

01
Weeks 1–2
Anatomical Adaptation
Sets: 3 · Reps: 12–20 RIR: 3–4 Rest: 90–120s Establish technique on all movements. Connective tissue conditioning. Higher reps build work capacity. No ego loads — feel the target muscle on every set.
02
Weeks 3–4
Hypertrophy — Load
Sets: 3–4 · Reps: 10–15 RIR: 2–3 Rest: 75–90s First meaningful load increases. Volume climbs on all priority muscles. Begin tracking weights — you should be adding load weekly. Introduce double progression.
03
Weeks 5–6
Hypertrophy — Volume
Sets: 4–5 · Reps: 8–12 RIR: 1–2 Rest: 60–75s Max adaptive volume for priority muscles. Introduce drop sets on trap shrugs and side delt raises. Closer to failure on all working sets. Expect soreness.
04
Weeks 7–8
Overreach + Deload
W7: Push to RIR 0–1 W8: 50% volume, same load Rest: 2–3 min W7 Week 7 = deliberate overreach at MRV. Week 8 = structured deload to allow supercompensation. Do NOT skip the deload. This is where gains consolidate.

4

3

2

1
RIR 1

0
RIR 0
Technical failure. Week 7 only. Could not do another clean rep.
Training Split

Upper / Lower × 3

A 6-day Upper/Lower/Upper/Lower/Upper/Lower split ensures every priority muscle hits 2–3× per week with sufficient recovery. Upper days alternate between Push emphasis and Pull emphasis to manage fatigue. Priority muscles flagged in every session.
