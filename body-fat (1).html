◆ About Body Fat %
Body fat percentage is the proportion of your total bodyweight made up of fat tissue. Unlike weight or BMI, it distinguishes between fat mass and lean mass — making it one of the most useful metrics for health, performance, and physique goals.
Navy Tape Method
Developed by the US Navy for field assessments. Uses circumference measurements to estimate BF% via a validated logarithmic formula. Accuracy: ±3–4% vs DEXA.
Visual Assessment
Select the range that best matches your physique. Less precise at ±4–6%, but useful when measurements aren't available or as a quick sanity check.
BMI-Based Estimate
Uses the Deurenberg formula to convert your BMI into a BF% estimate. Requires only height, weight, age and sex. Accuracy: ±5–8%. Not reliable for very muscular individuals — BMI cannot distinguish fat from lean mass.
Gold Standard (Clinical)
DEXA scan (±1–2%), hydrostatic weighing, and Bod Pod offer clinical-grade accuracy. Typically $50–$200 at specialist facilities.
