Completed the 8 Weeks? Here's What to Do Next
The FRC/WHO framework on cycling this program
✅ Do This
Skip Phase 1 — Start at P2
Your nervous system has already mapped those passive ranges. Returning to 60–90s passive holds won't produce new adaptation. Begin your second cycle at Phase 2 (PAILs/RAILs) with heavier isometric contractions and longer holds.
📈 Progress the Load
Increase Intensity Each Cycle
Don't repeat the same weights and holds — that's maintenance, not adaptation. Cycle 2: add load where applicable. Cycle 3: increase PAILs/RAILs duration to 10–15s. Cycle 4+: prioritise P3–P4 end-range strength work.
🔄 Every 2nd Cycle
One Week P1 Deload First
Every second repeat (cycles 3, 5, 7…), spend the first week back in passive Foundation holds. This re-sensitises mechanoreceptors and gives connective tissue a recovery window before loading again.
🎯 Optional
Target Your Weak Links
After completion, you'll know your tightest areas. Double the weekly volume on those days — e.g. if hips are still limiting, run Monday's hip session twice per week alongside the regular schedule.

Technique Glossary
PAILs Progressive Angular Isometric Loading — contract away from the stretched direction at end range
RAILs Regressive Angular Isometric Loading — contract into the stretched direction at end range
CARs Controlled Articular Rotations — slow circles using full joint range under active control
Irradiation Squeezing surrounding muscles to create stability, allowing the target joint to relax further
Nerve Floss Gentle oscillating movement that slides the nerve through its sheath — reduces sciatic tension
End Range The furthest available position of a joint — training here builds lasting mobility gains
