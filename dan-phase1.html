Phase 1 — Foundation Block

Same structure as the standard Phase 1: two 30-minute full body sessions per week, alternating A and B. Every session hits push, pull, squat, hip extension, and core. The difference is in how we drive the posterior chain.

Instead of Romanian deadlifts or leg curls, Dan uses banded hip thrusts as the glute/hip extension anchor in both sessions. The band provides accommodating resistance — lighter at the bottom (where the hamstrings are most at risk under stretch load) and heavier at the top (where the glutes are in full contraction). This is a smart way to build hip extension strength without irritating the hamstrings.

Progression rule: Move to a heavier band, add a small plate across the hips, or increase reps when you can complete all sets with full glute squeeze and zero hamstring discomfort. Any hamstring sensation during the thrust = flag it immediately.

HS Modified Full Body 2× / Week 30 Min Banded Hip Thrust
Hamstring pain scale: 0 = nothing · 1–2 = mild awareness (acceptable, monitor) · 3+ = stop and tell your trainer. The banded hip thrust should feel like a glute exercise. If it pulls behind the knee or in the belly of the hamstring, reduce band tension and shorten the ROM at the bottom.

A
Workout A — Squat Focus
Full body session led by the goblet squat. Banded hip thrust replaces loaded hinge work as the posterior chain driver. Hits push, pull, squat, glutes, shoulder isolation, and core in 30 minutes.

Session A note: The banded hip thrust is the most important exercise in this session for Dan specifically. Set up the band just above the knees, lie back with upper back on the bench, and drive hips to full extension with a hard glute squeeze. If there is any hamstring pull at the bottom of the movement, reduce how far the hips drop — a shortened ROM is completely fine in Phase 1.

B
Workout B — Press Focus
Full body session anchored by the DB shoulder press and lat pulldown. The second banded hip thrust session of the week — same movement, builds the glute/hip extension pattern consistently. Unilateral leg work via reverse lunge with zero hamstring risk.

Session B note: This session's thrust setup is identical to Session A. The reverse lunge is safe — it loads the quad and glute, with the hamstrings acting passively. If Dan feels any pull during the lunge step, it's likely at a deeper range — shorten the step slightly. The goal is zero hamstring awareness across both sessions.
