What This Is

Built to run alongside everything

01
Plugin Architecture
Every session is designed to fit in 20–30 minutes and requires zero equipment. Do it before your gym session as a warm-up, after as a cool-down, or entirely on its own on a rest day. Nothing in this program interferes with your training — it only makes it better.
02
Progressive Like a Strength Program
Most mobility work is random. This isn't. Each week builds on the last — from passive holds to active range control to end-range strength. Week 8 looks nothing like Week 1 because your body has genuinely adapted.
03
Neurological, Not Just Mechanical
Flexibility without nervous system buy-in is useless. PAILs and RAILs isometrics (introduced in Phase 2) teach your brain to own the new range, not just passively reach it. That's the difference between real mobility and just being flexible when you're warm.
04
Breathwork Is the Protocol
Every stretch and hold has a breathing prescription. Exhale into the stretch — this isn't motivational language, it's physiology. Exhaling activates the parasympathetic system and reduces protective muscle tone, allowing genuine range improvements rather than just forcing tissue.

Progression Model

Four phases, each building the last

01
Weeks 1–2
Foundation
Passive holds: 60–90s Breathing: 5s in / 5s out RIR equivalent: gentle tension only Learn the positions. No forcing. Focus on breathing and finding the sensation. Your body is mapping new ranges — let it do so without threat.
02
Weeks 3–4
Loading
Passive + Active: 90–120s blocks Introduce PAILs / RAILs End of hold: 5s isometric contraction Begin contracting into the stretch (PAILs) and out of it (RAILs). This neurologically owns the range you've been building. Expect surprising muscle soreness from unfamiliar end-range loading.
03
Weeks 5–6
CARs & Control
Controlled Articular Rotations Active vs Passive range comparison 10s contractions, full circle work Introduce CARs — the full active range of motion at each joint. The gap between your passive and active range reveals where mobility is compensated. This phase closes that gap.
04
Weeks 7–8
Expression
End-range strength holds Loaded progressive stretches Flow sequences: 3–5 min continuous The final phase ties everything together into flowing movement. You're not holding positions — you're moving through them with control. This is where mobility becomes functional.

90/90 Hip Stretch

Static Hold

Coaching Cues
  • Sit tall — do not collapse to one side or posteriorly tilt
  • Both sit bones aiming for the floor equally

Breathe: Full exhale to melt into the position — do not tense up when you feel the limit

01 of 06
Thoracic Foam Roll
Extension and segment mobilisation of T4–T10
Joint Mobilisation
10 seg × 5s
Coaching Cues
  • Roll from T4 (base of shoulder blades) down to T10 (bottom of rib cage)
  • Pause on each segment — 5 full breaths, let gravity do the work
  • Hands behind head for support — do not pull on the neck
  • Hinge over the roller on exhale — extend the segment
  • No foam roller: use a rolled-up mat or two tennis balls taped together
Breathe: Exhale = extend over the roller. Each exhale deepens the mobilisation at that segment.
02 of 06
Thread the Needle
T-spine rotation — the most mobility-limited movement for desk workers and lifters
Active Mobility
10 each side
× 2 rounds
Coaching Cues
  • On hands and knees — hips square and still throughout
  • One arm threads under the body — shoulder goes to the floor
  • Opposite arm reaches to the ceiling on the return — maximise the rotation
  • The movement should come from the thoracic spine, not the lumbar
  • Hold the end position for 3 breaths before returning
Breathe: Exhale into the rotation — rotate further on each exhale
03 of 06
Doorway / Wall Chest Stretch
Pec minor and anterior capsule — reversed by overhead and row work
Static Hold
90s each side
Coaching Cues
  • Place forearm on wall at 90°, elbow at shoulder height
  • Rotate body away slowly until tension is felt across the front of the shoulder
  • Do not collapse the rib cage forward — brace lightly throughout
  • Three positions: elbow at 90°, 120°, and 60° to cover all pec fibres
Breathe: On each exhale, gently increase rotation — the stretch deepens with the body relaxed
04 of 06
Shoulder CARS (Controlled Articular Rotations)
The full available range of the glenohumeral joint — active and controlled
CARs
5 each way
each arm · slow
Coaching Cues
  • Standing — irradiate tension through the whole body (squeeze everything, not just the shoulder)
  • Move the arm through the absolute largest circle possible
  • Keep the scapula stable — the movement is at the shoulder joint, not the shoulder blade
  • Move at 10% of max speed — this is precision work, not swinging
  • In Phase 1, the circle may be small — that's your current range. It will grow.
Breathe: Continuous steady breathing — don't hold your breath during CARs
05 of 06
Lat & Side Body Stretch

Static Hold
90s each side
Coaching Cues
  • Hang from a bar one-handed, or use a pole/door — arm fully overhead
  • Allow the body to sag, feeling the stretch from armpit to hip
  • Gently rotate toward and away to explore different fibre angles
  • No bar: lie on your back, reach one arm overhead and cross the opposite leg over
Breathe: Lateral breathing — expand the rib cage sideways into the stretch on each inhale
06 of 06
Neck CARs
Cervical spine full circumduction — often the most neglected joint in the body
CARs
5 each way
Coaching Cues
  • Sitting or standing, shoulders completely still and relaxed
  • Draw the largest circle possible with the top of your head
  • Move slowly through any restriction — don't skip past tight spots

Coaching Cues

  • Nod yes and no slowly — this flossing the sciatic nerve through the spine

Seated Single-Leg Forward Fold
Isolated hamstring — removes the calf from the stretch equation
Static Hold
90s each side
Coaching Cues

  • Hold the shin, ankle or foot — not in a way that rounds the back aggressively
  • The stretch should be behind the knee and under the sitting bone
Breathe: Use a 4–7–8 pattern — 4s inhale, 7s hold, 8s exhale into fold

Neural mobilisation — not a stretch, a nerve glide through its full path
Neural Mobility
15 reps each side
slow, deliberate
Coaching Cues
  • Seated — extend one leg, dorsiflex foot (pull toes up) and extend neck back simultaneously
  • Then plantarflex foot (point toes) and flex chin to chest simultaneously
  • This is the nerve flossing motion — never painful, just sensation
  • If you feel shooting pain down the leg, ease off — see a physio before continuing

  • Stack the opposite hip down to the floor as you walk arms across
  • This is a decompression — the lower back should feel relief, not pressure

Active Mobility
10 full cycles

Coaching Cues

  • Wave the spine one vertebra at a time, sacrum to crown

📋
Weeks 1–2 Focus: In Phase 1, the priority is learning positions and establishing the breathing pattern. Do not chase depth. Do not force range. The body needs to map these positions neurologically before it will release tissue. Consistency across all 7 days matters far more than intensity in any single session.
Phase 2 — Loading  ·  Add PAILs/RAILs isometrics at end of holds  ·  5–10s contractions  ·  Begin to push the range actively  ·  Expect new muscle soreness
Same exercises, upgraded protocol
Keep every exercise from Weeks 1–2. Apply this protocol upgrade to each hold:

PAILs (Progressive Angular Isometric Loading): At the end of each passive hold, contract INTO the stretch for 5–10 seconds. For the 90/90 hip stretch, that means pressing the front shin into the floor. This contracts the muscle that is being lengthened, which is deeply unusual for the nervous system and forces it to accept the new range.

RAILs (Regressive Angular Isometric Loading): After the PAILs contraction, contract OUT of the stretch (opposing muscles) for 5–10 seconds. For the 90/90, that means lifting the front knee off the floor. This builds active strength in the new range.

After both contractions, relax and you should find an additional 5–15° of passive range available. This is neurological release.
Monday — Hips
90/90 + PAILs/RAILs
After 90s hold: press shin into floor 7s (PAILs) → relax 2s → lift knee off floor 7s (RAILs) → relax and explore new range for 30s. Repeat on the second side. Apply same pattern to pigeon and hip flexor stretch.
Tuesday — Thoracic
Thread the Needle + Loaded T-Spine
At the end of the rotation: press the threading arm gently into the floor (PAILs, 7s) → then reach the ceiling arm further (RAILs, 7s). Add a loaded book opener: lying on side, top arm sweeps open with a weight in hand.
Wednesday — Hamstrings
Supine Hamstring + PAILs/RAILs
After 90s hold: press the raised leg away from you into your hands (PAILs, 7s) → pull the leg closer (RAILs, 7s). Expect to find an additional 5–10° of passive range after. This is the key hamstring phase.
Thursday — Ankles
Add Banded Ankle Distraction
If you have a resistance band: loop it around the ankle, anchor low. The band pulls the ankle joint posteriorly, creating space in the joint capsule. This substantially amplifies the dorsiflexion stretch. 2 rounds × 90s each side.
Friday — Upper Flow
Shoulder CARs Loaded
Perform all 5 shoulder CARs holding a light weight (1–2kg). The load demands more active control through the range. Increase wall slide reps to 20.
Saturday — Lower Flow
Cossack Squat + Adductor PAILs
Wide-leg fold: press feet into the floor (PAILs, 7s) → pull feet together (RAILs, 7s). Cossack squat: add a 3s pause at the bottom. Hip 90/90 flow: aim to remove hand support by end of Week 4.
Sunday — Integration
Extend CAR Sequence to 7 joints
Add thoracic spine CARs and lumbar CARs to the joint sequence. Extend bear crawl to 4 rounds. Couch stretch: attempt full upright torso with no wall support by end of Week 4.
Phase 3 — CARs & Control  ·  Introduce full CARs at all joints  ·  10s contractions  ·  Compare passive vs active range  ·  Weighted mobility work begins
Closing the gap between passive and active range
By Week 5, your passive range has increased. Phase 3 is about making that range active and controllable. The gap between how far you can passively stretch and how far you can actively move reveals where mobility is compensated.

Monday: Add Hip CARs as the opening exercise — 5 full circles each way, each leg. Extend PAILs/RAILs contractions to 10s. Add a weighted goblet squat hold (8–12kg, 3 × 60s) as a loaded deep squat variation.

Tuesday: Add weighted thoracic CARs (hold 2kg plate overhead). Introduce the Bow & Arrow stretch — lying on side, grab top foot and pull into hip rotation. Add 3 × 5 Wall Angels with a foam roll behind the spine.

Wednesday: Add the Jefferson Curl — standing on a step, slowly roll down vertebra by vertebra with a light weight (2–5kg). This is loaded spinal flexion and the most effective posterior chain mobility exercise in existence. Start light.

Thursday: Weighted single-leg calf raises (3 × 15) added as ankle strengthening to lock in the dorsiflexion range. Add foot rolling on a lacrosse ball (3 min each foot).

Friday–Sunday: Begin linking exercises into 5-minute flows. The World's Greatest Stretch becomes continuous — move from one position to the next without resetting. Same with the CAR sequence.
Phase 4 — Expression  ·  End-range strength holds  ·  3–5 min continuous flows  ·  Loaded progressive stretches  ·  You own this range now
Movement, not just positions
Phase 4 removes the timer. Instead of holding positions, you're moving through them. The full 7-day structure now flows continuously — each session is one unbroken 20–25 minute sequence rather than isolated exercises.

Monday — Hip Flow (25 min): Hip CARs → 90/90 flow (both sides, no timer) → pigeon into hip flexor into deep squat → frog into cossack → close with 3 min supine breathwork. Move at your own pace — the sequence drives the timing.

Tuesday — Upper Flow (25 min): Neck CARs → shoulder CARs (weighted) → thread the needle → book opener → wall slide sequence → sleeper stretch → close with box breathing. No static holds — everything flows.

Wednesday — Posterior Chain Flow (25 min): Jefferson curl (5 × 5, light) → hamstring flow (both sides) → sciatic floss → child's pose → cat-cow (30 reps) → forward fold. Add a 90s hold wherever you feel the most restriction today.

Thursday — Ground-Up Stability (20 min): Foot rolling → ankle CARs → calf strength → balance challenge (eyes closed + towel) → this day becomes ankle and foot maintenance forever — do it even after the 8 weeks.

Friday–Sunday: Your body will tell you what it needs. These days become intuitive — use the exercises you've learned as the toolkit and build your own 20-minute practice. That's the goal of the whole program.
