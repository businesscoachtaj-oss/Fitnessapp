◆ Volume Landmarks Explained
MV
Maintenance Volume
The minimum sets per week needed to preserve existing muscle. Drop below this and you'll start losing gains.
MEV
Min Effective Volume
The least amount of training that still stimulates growth. Your starting point for a new training block.
MAV
Max Adaptive Volume
The sweet spot for growth. A range where you get the most adaptation per set. Aim to train within this band.
MRV
Max Recoverable Volume
The ceiling. Do more than this and recovery breaks down. Useful to know — never train above it long-term.
How to use these: Start a training block at MEV, progressively add 1–2 sets per muscle per week as you adapt, work up toward MRV over 4–6 weeks, then take a deload week back at MV before repeating. This is called Mesocycle Progression — the foundation of science-based hypertrophy programming (Israetel et al., Renaissance Periodization).
