Phase 1 — Foundation Block

The goal of Phase 1 is to build consistent training habits, establish strong movement patterns, and accumulate quality volume across all major muscle groups. With 30-minute sessions twice a week, every minute counts — the workouts are dense and efficient.

Workouts A and B alternate each week. Both are full-body, hitting push, pull, hinge, and squat patterns every session. Exercise and rep scheme variety keeps sessions engaging and drives broad adaptation.

Progression rule: When you can complete all reps with 2+ reps in reserve on every set, increase load by the smallest increment available next session.

Workout B — Hinge Focus
Full body session anchored by the DB deadlift. Shifts emphasis to the posterior chain, vertical pressing, and unilateral legs. Different exercises and rep ranges to Workout A — complementary, not duplicate.

Session B Focus: The DB deadlift is your main mover — keep the weights close to your legs, think "push the floor away" rather than "pull the weight up." On the DB shoulder press, don't lock out violently — controlled full extension. The face pull at the end is your shoulder health exercise — don't skip it.

Variety Note: Workouts A and B deliberately use different exercises to train the same patterns (e.g. goblet squat vs. DB deadlift, push-up vs. shoulder press, lateral raise vs. face pull). This variety is intentional — it builds broader movement competency and keeps training engaging. Over time, exercises will rotate to continue providing variety.
