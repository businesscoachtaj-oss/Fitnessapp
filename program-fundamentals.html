🫀
Cardio and Cardiovascular Health
150–300 minutes of moderate or 75–150 minutes of vigorous physical activity per week reduces cardiovascular disease risk by 35%, type 2 diabetes by 30%, and all-cause mortality by 30%. These are minimum effective doses — not aspirational targets.
💪
Muscle-Strengthening: Separate and Additive
Resistance training 2+ days per week independently reduces all-cause mortality, improves insulin sensitivity, preserves lean mass through ageing, and improves bone density. The WHO lists it separately to cardio because the benefits are distinct — you need both.
📈
The Dose-Response Relationship
Going from minimum to maximum (300 mins vigorous/week) provides additional reductions in cancer risk, depression, anxiety and metabolic syndrome. Above 300 minutes vigorous per week, the marginal return flattens. This program is built around the three evidence-supported tiers that capture distinct health benefit steps.
🏋️
Why These 9 Movements
The WHO prescribes strength work for "major muscle groups" — it doesn't name exercises. Sports science consistently identifies compound, multi-joint movements as the most efficient path to whole-body strength. These 9 patterns cover every major muscle group with the minimum number of exercises, and they directly mirror the strength standards on this platform.

Rest & Recovery

Rest Is Part of the Program

😴
Sleep
7–9 hours per night is where 90% of muscle protein synthesis occurs. Training stimulus without sleep is just stress. Poor sleep increases injury risk and kills motivation. It is the highest-leverage recovery tool available.
🚶
Active Rest Days
Rest days don't mean sedentary. 20–30 min walking on rest days accelerates recovery by increasing blood flow without adding training stress. It also counts toward your weekly cardio target. Walk every day.
🍽️
Protein
Muscles are built from protein. 1.6–2.2g per kg of bodyweight per day is the evidence-based range for muscle gain and retention during training. Spread across 3–4 meals. Timing matters far less than total daily intake.
💧
Hydration
Even 2% dehydration reduces strength output by 10–20%. Drink water consistently throughout the day — not just during training. Add electrolytes on heavy cardio days or in hot environments. Urine colour is the easiest marker.
